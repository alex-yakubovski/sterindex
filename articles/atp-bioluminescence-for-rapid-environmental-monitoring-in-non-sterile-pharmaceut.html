ATP Bioluminescence for Rapid Environmental Monitoring in Non-Sterile Pharmaceutical Manufacturing: A Quality by Design Approach.

Source PubMed
Published June 04, 2026
Indexed June 23, 2026

In a published study on PubMed from June 4, 2026, researchers explore the application of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) bioluminescence for swift environmental monitoring within non-sterile pharmaceutical manufacturing settings, using a Quality by Design approach.

The study focuses on the detection and quantification of ATP residues on surfaces as a surrogate marker for microbial contamination in various areas such as production facilities, packaging rooms, and warehouses. The method involves swabbing surfaces, transferring the sample to a luminometer that measures light produced by enzymatic reactions catalyzed by firefly luciferase, thus indicating ATP levels.

In their investigation, the team tested multiple sites in pharmaceutical manufacturing facilities, comparing the ATP bioluminescence results with traditional microbial testing methods. The study discovered that ATP bioluminescence yielded faster cycle times for environmental monitoring, providing real-time data within a few minutes compared to the hours required for conventional culture techniques.

The findings indicate that utilizing ATP bioluminescence could potentially improve compliance with good manufacturing practice (GMP) regulations and enhance the quality assurance process in non-sterile pharmaceutical manufacturing. However, it is essential to note that while ATP levels correlate with microbial contamination, they do not directly measure specific pathogens and should be interpreted alongside other data sources for a comprehensive understanding of the hygiene status within the facility.

The research does not delve into applications of this technique in sterile processing or surgical instrumentation settings but instead provides valuable insights into the potential benefits of adopting ATP bioluminescence for environmental monitoring in non-sterile pharmaceutical manufacturing environments following a Quality by Design approach.
