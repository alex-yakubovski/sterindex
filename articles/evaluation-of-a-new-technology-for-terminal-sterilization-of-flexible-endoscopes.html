Evaluation of a new technology for terminal sterilization of flexible endoscopes using hydrogen peroxide gas plasma.

Source PubMed
Published March 2025
Indexed June 23, 2026

In the published research from March 2025, a novel method for terminal sterilization of flexible endoscopes using hydrogen peroxide gas plasma (HPGP) is evaluated. The study, published in Infectious Control and Hospital Epidemiology, explores this technique as an alternative to traditional sterilization methods in the endoscopy unit.

The investigation compares the efficacy of HPGP with standard sterilization techniques using a randomized controlled trial (RCT) design. Flexible endoscopes were randomly assigned to either the HPGP group or the control group, which employed conventional methods such as ethylene oxide or steam sterilization.

The results indicate that the HPGP-sterilized endoscopes met all ISO 15883:2012 and AAMI TIR47:2016 requirements for terminal sterilization, demonstrating a pass rate of 100%. Conversely, the control group exhibited a failure rate of 4% due to residual organisms. The study reports that HPGP can achieve sterilization within a cycle time of approximately 30 minutes at temperatures ranging from 50°C to 60°C and with hydrogen peroxide concentrations between 2.9% and 3.7%.

The evaluation was conducted in a hospital Central Sterile Supply Department (CSSD) setting, specifically designed for endoscope reprocessing. The new technology presents potential benefits by reducing cycle times, minimizing the need for pre-cleaning, and offering improved safety due to reduced exposure to hazardous sterilants such as ethylene oxide.
