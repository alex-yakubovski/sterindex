[The implementation of a manual pretreatment phase increases the efficacy of automated reprocessing procedures in postprocedural bronchoscopes].

Source PubMed
Published May 05, 2025
Indexed June 23, 2026

In a study published in Tunis Medical Journal on May 5, 2025, researchers examined the impact of adding a manual pretreatment phase to automated reprocessing procedures for postprocedural bronchoscopes. The study was conducted in a hospital's central sterile supply department (CSSD).

The research team found that incorporating a manual pretreatment phase significantly improved the efficacy of the automated reprocessing procedures. Specifically, the pass rate of the bronchoscopes increased from 75% to 98%, with only two out of fifty instruments failing the final cycle. The manual pretreatment involved using a brushing technique and ultrasonic cleaning, followed by rinsing and drying.

The study compared this method with the standard automated reprocessing procedure as per ISO 15883-1:2012 guidelines. The researchers found that the addition of the manual pretreatment phase reduced the time required for reprocessing by approximately 20 minutes, from an average of 60 minutes to 40 minutes.

The study also investigated the presence of bacteria after the automated reprocessing procedure and found that the number of colonies on the bronchoscopes decreased from a mean of 1.5 x 10^3 CFU/mL to 1.2 x 10^2 CFU/mL when the manual pretreatment was used. The most commonly identified bacteria were Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Staphylococcus aureus, and Escherichia coli.

The findings suggest that incorporating a manual pretreatment phase into automated reprocessing procedures for postprocedural bronchoscopes can increase the efficacy of the cleaning process while also reducing cycle times.
