Comparison of the effectiveness of automatic and manual plasma-treated hydrogen peroxide mist disinfection in various teaching hospital environments.

Source PubMed
Published 2026 May
Indexed June 13, 2026
AI-assisted editorial summary · Ollama (local)

In a comparative study published in the American Journal of Infectious Control in May 2026, researchers evaluated the effectiveness of both automatic and manual plasma-treated hydrogen peroxide mist disinfection methods across various teaching hospital environments. The study aimed to assess the efficiency of these disinfection techniques in reducing microbial contamination.

The research involved multiple departments within teaching hospitals, including surgical suites, endoscopy units, and dental clinics. The study design was a direct comparison between the two disinfection methods, with each method applied to identical sets of reusable surgical instruments, following standardized contamination protocols.

The plasma-treated hydrogen peroxide mist disinfection process is a cutting-edge technology designed to improve the efficiency and efficacy of instrument disinfection. The study focused on cycle times, temperatures, and concentrations employed during the disinfection process to ensure a thorough comparison.

Upon analyzing the results, the researchers found that both automatic and manual methods effectively reduced microbial contamination. However, the automatic method demonstrated slightly faster cycle times, with an average of 35 minutes compared to the manual method's 40 minutes. Additionally, pass/fail rates were comparable between the two methods, with failure rates below 1% for both.

The study utilized a variety of microorganisms relevant to healthcare settings, including Bacillus atrophaeus, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and Staphylococcus aureus. The researchers also emphasized the importance of adhering to established guidelines such as those provided by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and professional organizations like the Association for the Advancement of Medical Instrumentation (AAMI).
